98-177: Student-Taught Course | Building Personal Websites

This course, like many other StuCos, is pass-fail. Every week students will be asked to update their personal website from the start of the course. Students are asked to put in at least 1 hour of work each week into their website. Each website will be published on GitHub pages and will be checked to see if enough effort has been put into it each week by looking at the page commits.

All assignments are due Thursdays at 6:30pm unless otherwise stated. Students are asked at the beginning of the semester to submit a link to the repository responsible for holding their personal website. Every week, students should make at least one push updating their website with content from the previous week. The focus of the course should not be about passing, but making a website you can be proud of.

At the start of each class, attendance will be taken. The StuCo guidelines state that students should be allowed on average 2 absences per semester. Thus, students may miss at most 2 classes and are allowed to go 2 weeks without updating their site. Keep in mind that units build upon each other. Students are responsible for reviewing lecture slides to keep up with material missed in order not to get left behind.
