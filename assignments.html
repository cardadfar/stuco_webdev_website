98-331: Student-Taught Course | Animation & Video Editing

The course, like many other StuCos, is pass-fail and consists of a Midterm, Final, and series of homeworks referred to as ‘quick-edits’. There will be a total of 8 quick-edits during the semester where students will have to render a short 10-20 second video utilizing concepts learned from the most recent lecture. Quick-edits are graded based on completion and are used to indicate whether a student understands how to correctly implement a certain effect or technique.

The Midterm will be an extended quick-edit of about 30-45 seconds where students will create a video of their choice using video-editing, kinetic typography, and motion graphics. The Midterm demonstrates a student’s understanding in video-editing practices. The Final will be a video around 1 minute long. Any video format is acceptable: animation, video-editing, live-action, motion graphics, etc. Students will document their process, from storyboarding, to animatics, to the final video.

All assignments are due Thursdays at 4:30pm unless otherwise stated. Assignments are to be submitted to the StuCo AVE (Animation Video-Editing) Google Drive folder. You must be logged into your andrew accounts to have access to the folder.

Label your submissions '[Last Name] Quick Edit [#]' and attach it to the corresponding assignment folder.

Each assignment is used to mark attendance for the previous class. The StuCo guidelines state that students should be allowed on average 2 absences per semester. With the 7 total quick-edits, students may miss up to 2 quick-edits (excluding Midterm & Final) during the course. Attendance is not marked on days where no assignment is due.
