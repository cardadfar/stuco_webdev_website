98-177: Student-Taught Course | Building Personal Websites

The great thing about web development is that you have the web to help you! Online you can find hundreads of resources to help you. Whether its trying to debug a certain element or looking for inspiration to expand your website, everything is out there to help you. Even after the course's end, you can use these resources to go further into web development.

Codeacademy
A great place to start if you ever want to learn a new programminf language. Codeacademy has courses in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and many more web development tools that can help you expand your site.
Codeacademy
W3 Schools
A very vivid online doccumentation of how web development works. w3School provides definitions, examples, and working demos of all of the most common (and not-so-common) elements of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more. With w3Schools, you never need to memorize something, since its doccumentation will always be there.
w3Schools
Stack Overflow
If you've got a question, it's likely Stack Overflow's got an answer. With millions of users swarming the site daily ready to answer questions, you can anways be sure your question is out there already answered before you even ask it. If you're ever wondering why that div you drew isn't displaying on your site or why you element isn't moving when you hover over it, it's likely that Stack Overflow knows why.
